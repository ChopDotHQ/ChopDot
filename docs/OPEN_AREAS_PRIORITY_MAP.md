## FACTS

- ChopDot now has a coherent current doctrine:
  - shared commitment kernel
  - coordination first
  - proof second
  - subscription-led now
  - API-ready now, API-product later
- The strongest current wedge remains:
  - group deposits before execution
  - chaptered closeout / reliable closure
- The biggest remaining weakness is:
  - proof, not theory
- Teddy is currently on the critical implementation path.
- Founder-side parallel work is now documented separately.

## INFERENCES

- There are no longer major missing categories.
- There are still many open areas, but they are not equally important.
- The most dangerous mistake now would be to treat all open areas as equally urgent.
- The correct move is:
  - prove the kernel
  - prove the wedge
  - prove willingness-to-pay
  - harden the operating perimeter
  - only then expand into rails, builders, agents, and tokenized rights

## ASSUMPTIONS

- "Open" means unresolved, unproven, incomplete, or not yet operationalized.
- This map is for current decision-making, not a final permanent roadmap.
- Priority is based on:
  - dependency
  - business importance
  - proof value
  - risk reduction

## Status Labels

### Active now

Must be worked now because they directly affect product proof, founder proof, or launch readiness.

### Supporting current work

Helpful now, but not on the tightest critical path.

### Deferred

Important later, but not worth front-loading before proof exists.

### Rejected for now

Not aligned with current posture and should not quietly re-enter scope.

## Priority Legend

- `P0` = critical path, blocking proof
- `P1` = immediate founder/company proof
- `P2` = important hardening, should begin soon
- `P3` = useful supporting work
- `P4` = later / deferred
