ChopDot requests treasury support to maintain and ship measurable Polkadot-native collaboration-finance improvements with milestone-gated accountability. This proposal is scoped to realistic delivery with explicit KPIs and monthly reporting.

## 3. Why We Built This
Shared finance for groups is still fragmented, opaque, and fragile. We built ChopDot to provide practical group coordination for expenses and settlements, with optional on-chain verifiability and identity-aware trust progression.

## 4. Why Now
Polkadot is moving toward application-centric products and system-chain utility. The timing is strong for user-facing apps that turn chain infrastructure into daily utility. ChopDot is built to meet that shift now, not later.

## 5. Why This Is Not Just Another Split App
Typical split apps optimize convenience in closed systems. ChopDot is building open coordination finance:
- Wallet-native and chain-aware transaction flows.
- Verifiable settlement history where users need proof.
- Identity/personhood compatibility path for trust-sensitive groups.
- Governance-compatible reporting for communities and network-native organizations.

## 6. Strategic Alignment
### 6.1 Polkadot App Fit
ChopDot is complementary to the Polkadot App: the Polkadot App can serve broad onboarding and wallet access, while ChopDot serves high-frequency collaborative finance workflows for groups.

### 6.2 Personhood / People Chain Fit
ChopDot is building toward person-aware trust context for group finance. The product roadmap includes People Chain-compatible identity signals and clear disclosure when identity guarantees are weak or absent.

### 6.3 Network State Fit
Network-native communities need operational finance coordination: who paid, who owes, what settled, and what remains. ChopDot targets this coordination layer directly.

### 6.4 JAM Path
ChopDot is shipping utility on current Polkadot rails while keeping architecture modular for JAM-era evolution. We do not depend on JAM launch timing; we design for compatibility and progressive migration.
